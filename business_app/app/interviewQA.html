InterviewIQ

The following are common interview questions that many interviewers use to get to know their applicants during a face to face interview and sometimes a phone interview. Save your responses and edit them as you go so they can evolve into thought through and sophisticated answers. If you're stuck, feel free to check out the helpful tips.


Tell me about yourself.

Tip: This question is super broad for a reason. It can either make you or break you, so make sure to prepare a thoughtful response. Remember, this should feel more like a conversation than a question-answer time. Don’t forget to ask the interviewer questions, too. Do: Focus on your career. How did you get here? Where do you want to go? Answer this question by highlighting why you are the best candidate. Spend about 3 minutes on this response. Don’t: Tell your life story. Don’t ramble on. Don’t talk about your pets.

What are some of your short and long term goals?

Why should we hire you? What do you bring to the table?

Why do you want to work here? What do you know about this company and position?

What are you expecting as far as compensation?

What are your strengths and weaknesses?

Why do you want to leave your current job? Why did you leave your previous job?

How would your previous/current boss or coworkers describe you?

Tell me about a time you were in the midst of a stressful situation, and how did you deal with it?

What are your goals for this position?
